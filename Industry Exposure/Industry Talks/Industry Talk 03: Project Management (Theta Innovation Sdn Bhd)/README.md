## 📖 Overview

The industrial talk focused on project management practices in real-world system development environments. The session was delivered by Ts. Abdul Alim Abdul Mutallib, who shared professional experience in managing software projects within Theta Innovation Sdn Bhd.

The talk emphasized the use of **Agile methodology**, particularly the **Scrum framework**, in managing modern software development projects. Agile practices allow teams to work iteratively, communicate effectively, and deliver high-quality systems efficiently.

One of the highlighted projects was **SAKUPAY**, an e-wallet application developed within six months. The project demonstrated strong teamwork, efficient task distribution, and continuous collaboration among team members.

Key Agile practices discussed included:
- Sprint planning and task distribution
- Daily Scrum meetings (15-minute progress updates)
- Use of GitHub Kanban boards for task tracking
- Role definitions (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Development Team)
- Sprint retrospectives for continuous improvement

---

## 💭 Reflection

The session provided valuable insights into how real-world software projects are managed in the industry. Agile methodology was highlighted as a key approach in ensuring efficient teamwork, structured workflows, and continuous improvement.

The importance of communication and teamwork was strongly emphasized, as clear coordination among team members ensures successful project delivery. Leadership plays a crucial role in guiding teams, monitoring progress, and maintaining productivity.

Time management and discipline were also highlighted as essential skills in the industry. While modern tools such as AI can assist in development, strong fundamental knowledge and critical thinking remain necessary to ensure code quality and understanding.

Overall, the session enhanced awareness of industry practices and reinforced the importance of developing both technical and soft skills for future career readiness in system development and project management.
